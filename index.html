Brand Identity — Univerce

Velvet rope, loud room.

Univerce checks your student ID at the door. Everything past that door is tribal, loud, and unfiltered. Composed at the threshold, chaotic in the room — that tension is the entire brand, and every choice below is built to hold both at once.

01

Positioning

Univerce is the only feed that gates entry with a real, verified student ID. That single act of gatekeeping is what makes the noise inside mean something — a reaction from your campus carries weight because the person reacting is real, enrolled, and stuck at the same college as you. The brand should always read as two temperatures at once: composed at the door, unfiltered in the room. Gold never has to say "exclusive" out loud. The checkmark already said it.

Verified, not vague

Every account ties to a real student ID. Status is proven, never implied — the brand doesn't oversell exclusivity because the verification gate already delivers it.

Campus is a jersey

Inter-college rivalry — fests, sports days, bragging rights — is already real social currency. Univerce just gives it a live scoreboard.

Status is earned, not bought

Streaks, badges, and campus rank come from activity, not payment. In this brand, gold always means "you did something" — never "you paid something."

Confident over corporate

The app talks like a sharp senior, not a brand account. Dry, direct, in on the joke — never forced slang, never exclamation-point enthusiasm.

02

The voice carries the same duality as the visuals: restrained when it's talking about status or verification, loose and needling when it's talking about rivalry.

Color system

Six colors, three jobs. Obsidian and Bone build the quiet container. Gold marks anything earned or verified — used sparingly, never as decoration. Coral and Violet exist for exactly one purpose: rivalry.

The rivalry system: every campus that crosses a verified-user threshold gets auto-assigned Coral or Violet — arbitrarily, like a jersey color. The assignment doesn't need to mean anything; the point is that your campus has one and the rival campus has the other. This is the only place in the identity where color turns tribal and personal, which is exactly why it should never leak into general UI — it stays scarce so it stays meaningful.

05

Logo & symbol

All-lowercase wordmark — confident, not shouting. The symbol is a standalone verified mark that can run alone as an app icon or profile ring.

univerce
Verified mark — app icon, profile ring, badge
Rivalry pairing — two campuses, two rings

Directional concept, not a production file. For final vector work: the verified mark is designed to eventually sit in the wordmark's negative space — most naturally replacing the tittle on the second "i" — but that placement needs a type designer working in the actual font outlines, not raw SVG text positioning.

06

Iconography

Outline is the resting state of exclusivity. Fill is the reward state of activity.

2px stroke weight throughout. Rounded line caps keep the resting outline set approachable rather than clinical; corner joins stay sharp at 90°+ angles so the set doesn't drift bubbly or toy-like. Icons only fill when they represent something active or earned — a verified badge, a lit streak flame — never as a default decorative style.

08

Motion

The door moves slowly. The room bounces.

Verification & status

Ease-out, 400–600ms, settles without overshoot. Onboarding, ID verification, badge reveals — anything tied to trust should feel deliberate, never snappy.

Reactions & rivalry

Spring physics, 150–250ms, slight overshoot on entry. Reactions, streak increments, live score updates — anything tied to energy should feel a little too fast, on purpose.
